Spaghetti Carbonara

Cooking Time: 25 minutes
Difficulty: Medium
👁️ 892 people have viewed this recipe

🥘 Ingredients

400g spaghetti pasta
200g guanciale or pancetta, diced
4 large egg yolks
100g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
100g Parmesan cheese, grated
2 teaspoons black pepper
Salt to taste
2 liters water for pasta

👨‍🍳 Cooking Steps

1

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente (usually 9-10 minutes). Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.

2

While the pasta cooks, cut the guanciale into small cubes. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and cook the guanciale until crispy, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from heat.

3

In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, grated Pecorino Romano, Parmesan cheese, and freshly ground black pepper. The mixture should be creamy.

4

Add the drained hot pasta to the skillet with guanciale (off heat). Slowly pour the egg mixture over the pasta while continuously stirring to coat evenly.

5

If the sauce seems too thick, add reserved pasta water a little at a time, stirring continuously. The residual heat will cook the eggs without scrambling them.

6

Divide the spaghetti carbonara into serving bowls and serve immediately while hot. Garnish with additional cheese and freshly cracked black pepper to taste.
